When to Use Sunscreen

You should apply sunscreen if the UV index is high, especially during peak hours of sunlight. Sunscreen helps protect your skin from harmful UV rays that can cause skin damage and increase the risk of skin cancer. It's especially important to use sunscreen when spending extended periods outdoors or when participating in outdoor activities.

In addition, reapply sunscreen every two hours or more often if you’re sweating or swimming. Even on cloudy days, UV rays can penetrate the clouds and harm your skin, so it’s essential to protect your skin every day.

Understanding the UV Index

The UV index is a measure of the strength of ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun. It helps you understand the potential risk of skin damage at different levels of UV exposure. The higher the UV index, the greater the risk of harm to your skin and eyes.

Tips for Protecting Your Skin

  • Apply sunscreen with at least SPF 30.
  • Wear a wide-brimmed hat and sunglasses.
  • Seek shade, especially during peak sun hours (10 AM - 4 PM).
  • Reapply sunscreen every two hours, or more often if swimming or sweating.
